*Strategic Document 6 of 8 | Last Updated: January 2026*

## Executive Summary

Before committing $100K+ to development, invest **$5-8K and 4-6 weeks** in validation activities to de-risk critical assumptions. This plan defines specific validation activities, success criteria, and decision points.

**Validation Budget**: $5,000-8,000
**Validation Timeline**: 4-6 weeks
**Go/No-Go Decision**: Week 6

## 1. User Interview Plan

### Objective
Validate that the target audience:
1. Experiences the problem we're solving
2. Would use the proposed solution
3. Would pay for premium features

### Target Segments

| Segment | # Interviews | Profile |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| First-time homeowners | 10 | Bought home in last 2 years, ages 28-40 |
| DIY enthusiasts | 5 | Self-identify as DIY, have done home repairs |
| Experienced homeowners | 5 | 5+ years in current home, some repairs done |
| **Total** | **20** | |

### Recruitment Strategy

| Channel | Expected Yield | Cost |
|---------|---------------|------|
| Nextdoor posts (Austin) | 5-8 participants | Free |
| Facebook groups (Austin homeowners, DIY) | 4-6 participants | Free |
| User Interviews.com | 6-8 participants | $300-500 |
| Personal network referrals | 2-4 participants | Free |

**Incentive**: $25 Amazon gift card per 30-minute interview
**Total recruitment budget**: $800-1,000

### Interview Guide

#### Section 1: Problem Validation (10 min)

1. "Tell me about the last time something broke or stopped working in your home."
   - Follow-up: What did you do first? How did you figure out what was wrong?

2. "How do you typically handle home maintenance tasks?"
   - Follow-up: Do you have a schedule? How do you know when things need service?

3. "What's your biggest frustration when something goes wrong at home?"
   - Listen for: cost concerns, uncertainty, time wasted, bad contractors

4. "Walk me through the last time you called a professional. How did you decide it was necessary?"

#### Section 2: Current Behavior (5 min)

5. "What tools or apps do you currently use to manage your home?"
   - Follow-up: What do you like/dislike about them?

6. "When you search for how to fix something, where do you go first?"
   - Follow-up: What's frustrating about that process?

7. "Have you ever followed online advice that didn't work for your situation? Tell me about it."

#### Section 3: Solution Validation (10 min)

*Show concept mockups or describe the app*

8. "If an app knew the specifics of your home—your appliances, your pipes, your water source—and could give you personalized troubleshooting advice, how useful would that be?"
   - Follow-up: What would make you trust that advice?

9. "Would you take 2 minutes to set up a profile of your home if it meant getting personalized answers?"
   - Follow-up: What information would you be comfortable sharing? Uncomfortable?

10. "If the app told you 'you need a professional for this' and offered to connect you, would you use that?"

#### Section 4: Willingness to Pay (5 min)

11. "How much do you typically spend on home maintenance per year?"

12. "If this app saved you one unnecessary service call per year—maybe $150-300—would you pay $30/year for it?"
    - Follow-up: What would need to be true for you to pay?

13. "What features would make you pay vs. use for free?"

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | How Measured |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Problem resonance | 80%+ confirm frustration | "Yes" to Q3 frustration |
| Current behavior validates gap | 60%+ cite personalization issue | Mention in Q7 |
| Solution interest | 70%+ show enthusiasm | Positive response to Q8 |
| Profile willingness | 70%+ would complete | "Yes" to Q9 |
| Willingness to pay $30/yr | 40%+ would pay | Affirmative to Q12 |
| Trust in professional referral | 60%+ would use | "Yes" to Q10 |

### Interview Analysis Template

For each interview, document:
- [ ] Problem confirmed (Y/N)
- [ ] Current solution frustrations
- [ ] Profile willingness (Y/N)
- [ ] Solution excitement (1-5)
- [ ] Would pay (Y/N, at what price)
- [ ] Key quotes
- [ ] Unexpected insights

## 2. Service Provider Interview Plan

### Objective
Validate that local service providers:
1. Would join the referral network
2. Would pay $35+ per qualified lead
3. Have concerns we need to address

### Target Providers (Austin, TX)

| Category | # Interviews | Target Companies |
|----------|--------------|------------------|
| HVAC | 5 | Small/medium local companies |
| Plumbing | 3 | Owner-operated or small firms |
| Electrical | 2 | Licensed local electricians |
| **Total** | **10** | |

### Recruitment Strategy

1. **Cold outreach**: LinkedIn, Google Maps listings, Yelp
2. **Referral**: Ask interviewed providers for intros to others
3. **Chamber of Commerce**: Austin business directory

**Approach**: Phone call or in-person visit (more effective than email)

**Incentive**: No incentive needed (business opportunity pitch)

### Interview Guide

#### Section 1: Current Lead Generation (10 min)

1. "How do you currently get new customers?"
   - Listen for: referrals, Thumbtack, Angi, Google ads, yard signs

2. "What's your average cost to acquire a new customer?"
   - Follow-up: How much do you pay per lead on Thumbtack/Angi?

3. "What frustrates you most about current lead sources?"
   - Listen for: lead quality, competition, cost, no-shows

4. "What makes a 'good lead' vs. a 'bad lead'?"

#### Section 2: Lead Quality Discussion (5 min)

5. "If a lead came from a homeowner who already tried to fix the problem themselves and determined they need a pro, would that be higher quality?"
   - Follow-up: Why or why not?

6. "How much more would you pay for a lead where you already know the problem details?"

#### Section 3: Platform Interest (10 min)

*Describe the app and referral model*

7. "If we sent you leads from homeowners who used our troubleshooting app and determined they need professional help—would you want those leads?"

8. "Would you pay $35 per qualified lead?"
   - Follow-up: What would make it worth that price?
   - If no: What would you pay?

9. "How many leads per month would be valuable for your business?"

10. "What would make you NOT want to work with us?"

#### Section 4: Logistics (5 min)

11. "How would you want to receive leads?" (call, text, app, email)

12. "What information about the homeowner/problem would you need?"

13. "Would you want to be one of multiple providers or exclusive for your area?"

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | How Measured |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Interest in joining | 70%+ interested | Positive response to Q7 |
| Would pay $35/lead | 50%+ accept | "Yes" to Q8 |
| Minimum acceptable price | Average ≥$25 | Responses to Q8 follow-up |
| Lead volume interest | Average 10+/month | Q9 responses |
| No deal-breaker concerns | <2 fundamental objections | Q10 analysis |

### Provider Interview Summary Template

For each interview, document:
- [ ] Company name, size, years in business
- [ ] Current lead sources and costs
- [ ] Pain points with current sources
- [ ] Interest level (1-5)
- [ ] Acceptable price per lead
- [ ] Desired lead volume
- [ ] Preferred contact method
- [ ] Concerns/objections
- [ ] Would refer other providers? (Y/N)

## 3. Landing Page Test Plan

### Objective
Validate market demand through a landing page that:
1. Measures interest (email signups)
2. Tests messaging variants
3. Captures willingness-to-pay signal

### Landing Page Structure

**Hero Section**:
- Headline: Test variants (see below)
- Subhead: "Know your home. Fix it yourself."
- CTA: "Get Early Access" (email capture)

**Problem Section**:
- 3 pain points with icons
- "Sound familiar?" transition

**Solution Section**:
- How it works (3 steps)
- Screenshot mockups

**Pricing Section**:
- Show $29.99/year price
- List free vs premium features
- "Reserve your spot" CTA

**Social Proof** (if available):
- Testimonial quotes (from user interviews)
- "Join 500+ homeowners" (social proof counter)

### Messaging Variants (A/B Test)

| Variant | Headline | Hypothesis |
|---------|----------|------------|
| A (Control) | "Fix It Yourself with Advice That Knows Your Home" | Clear value prop |
| B (Problem) | "Stop Paying for Repairs You Could Handle" | Pain-focused |
| C (Savings) | "Save $500/Year on Home Maintenance" | Financial benefit |

### Traffic Sources

| Channel | Budget | Expected Visitors | Expected CAC |
|---------|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| Facebook/Instagram ads | $1,500 | 3,000-5,000 | $0.30-0.50 |
| Google Ads (home repair keywords) | $1,000 | 500-800 | $1.25-2.00 |
| Reddit (r/homeowners, r/DIY) | $500 | 500-1,000 | $0.50-1.00 |
| **Total** | **$3,000** | **4,000-6,800** | |

### Conversion Funnel

```
Visitors → Email Signup → Pricing Page View → "Reserve Spot"
  100%   →     8-15%    →      50%          →     30%
```

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Excellent |
|--------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Visitor → Email signup | 5% | 10% | 15% |
| Email → Pricing page view | 30% | 50% | 70% |
| Pricing view → Reserve | 15% | 30% | 50% |
| **End-to-end conversion** | **0.2%** | **1.5%** | **5%** |

### Tracking & Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| Carrd or Webflow | Landing page builder | $20-50/month |
| Google Analytics 4 | Traffic & conversion tracking | Free |
| Hotjar | Heatmaps, scroll tracking | Free tier |
| Mailchimp | Email capture | Free tier |
| Google Ads | Paid traffic | $1,000 budget |

### Landing Page Test Timeline

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Build landing page, set up tracking |
| 2 | Launch ads, monitor initial traffic |
| 3 | Analyze early results, adjust targeting |
| 4 | Run A/B test on headline variants |

## 6. Go/No-Go Decision Framework

### Scoring Matrix

| Criterion | Weight | Score 1-5 | Weighted |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Problem resonance (user interviews) | 25% | ___ | ___ |
| Solution interest (user interviews) | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| Willingness to pay (user + landing page) | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| Provider interest | 15% | ___ | ___ |
| Landing page conversion | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| **Total** | 100% | | **___** |

### Decision Thresholds

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 4.0+ | **GO**: Proceed to development |
| 3.0-3.9 | **CONDITIONAL**: Address weak areas, re-validate |
| 2.0-2.9 | **PIVOT**: Major changes needed before proceeding |
| <2.0 | **KILL**: Fundamental assumptions invalid |

### Score Interpretation Guide

**Problem resonance (1-5)**:
- 5: 90%+ confirm problem, strong emotional response
- 4: 80%+ confirm, clear frustration
- 3: 60-80% confirm, moderate interest
- 2: 40-60% confirm, mixed signals
- 1: <40% confirm, no clear problem

**Solution interest (1-5)**:
- 5: "I need this now" - would sign up today
- 4: Strong interest, asks when it's available
- 3: Interested but skeptical
- 2: Mild interest, many concerns
- 1: Not interested or confused

**Willingness to pay (1-5)**:
- 5: 50%+ would pay $30/yr, landing page converts >10%
- 4: 40%+ would pay, landing page converts 5-10%
- 3: 30%+ would pay, landing page converts 2-5%
- 2: <30% would pay, landing page converts <2%
- 1: No one willing to pay

**Provider interest (1-5)**:
- 5: 80%+ would join, accept $35/lead
- 4: 60-80% would join at $30-35
- 3: 50%+ interested at $25+
- 2: Mixed interest, price concerns
- 1: Little interest or major objections

**Landing page conversion (1-5)**:
- 5: >15% email conversion
- 4: 10-15% email conversion
- 3: 5-10% email conversion
- 2: 2-5% email conversion
- 1: <2% email conversion

## 7. Post-Validation Actions

### If GO:
1. Share validation results with stakeholders/investors
2. Finalize MVP scope based on learnings
3. Begin development immediately
4. Maintain email list for beta launch

### If CONDITIONAL:
1. Identify specific weak areas
2. Design focused follow-up validation
3. Set 2-week timeline for re-validation
4. Adjust strategy based on learnings

### If PIVOT:
1. Analyze what didn't work
2. Identify alternative approaches:
   - Different target audience
   - Different value proposition
   - Different business model
3. Design new validation for pivot

### If KILL:
1. Document learnings for future reference
2. Notify email list subscribers
3. Consider selling email list/domain
4. Move on to next opportunity
